The dojo道場

We preserve these arts as they were handed down: true to their martial roots in Japan, alive in the hands of the modern practitioner.

Miami Budokan is a not-for-profit martial arts and cultural academy housed in the Japan Cultural Center, in Miami's Coral Terrace neighborhood. Our students study the sword seriously and without shortcuts: certified instructors, recognized rank, and a curriculum that connects every class to centuries of practice. Members test and rank through the All United States Kendo Federation.

The arts剣

Two traditions. One floor.

居合道iaidō

ZNKR Seitei Iaidō

To draw, to cut, and to return the sword with grace, power, and a calm mind.

Iaidō is the traditional Japanese art of drawing the sword. Practiced through solo forms called kata, it develops precision, posture, and composure under pressure. We teach the standardized Zen Nippon Kendō Renmei (ZNKR) curriculum, the modern international standard used for testing and promotion by the All United States Kendo Federation.

  • ZNKR Seitei curriculum
  • Rank testing via AUSKF
古流koryū

Musō Shinden-ryū

The classical root of the modern art.

Alongside the modern curriculum, we practice Musō Shinden-ryū, a koryū, or "ancient style," of iaidō handed down from teacher to student for generations. Studying a classical school deepens the seitei forms and connects our students directly to the roots of the art in Japan. The chart below traces that line, name by name, from Nakayama Hakudo to our teachers.

Philosophy心

What the sword teaches.

克己心

Self-mastery

Kokkishin

A spirit of self-denial: setting aside ego, preconception, and unchecked emotion so the mind stays clear and free. Kokkishin is the heart of how we train, given to the dojo in 2014 in Kaneda Sensei's own brush, signed and sealed, shown here.

忍耐

Perseverance

Nintai

Endurance, patience, and steady effort: in a difficult kata, in a long practice, and in daily life beyond the dojo floor.

武道館

Community

Budokan

A budokan is a hall of martial ways, and a family. Each student pursues their own improvement as part of a worldwide community of practitioners.

Membership道

Month to month. No contracts.

Dojo fees are collected monthly and include all general classes and open practices. There is nothing to sign and nothing to cancel. Membership is month to month, and family discounts are available for multiple members of the same household.

All members register with the All United States Kendo Federation (about $100/year) for insurance, rank testing, and seminar participation.
